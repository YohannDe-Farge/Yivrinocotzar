Welcome to Yivrinocotzar, an Institute for the Comparative Study and Preservation of the Jewish Languages Yivrinocotzar is hereby established as a scholarly institution devoted to the collection, study, and elucidation of the varied linguistic heritage of the Jewish people throughout the Dispersion, embracing not only the more familiar tongues of Hebrew, Yiddish, Ladino, and Aramaic, but also the lesser-recorded idioms of our communities in Europe, Asia, and the Eretz Israel. It is the object of this Institute to regard these forms of speech not as isolated curiosities, but as integral expressions of a continuous historical and cultural tradition, deserving of careful preservation and learned inquiry. In furtherance of this purpose, Yivrinocotzar shall apply itself to the gathering of texts, oral traditions, and documentary materials; to the comparative examination of linguistic structures; and to the advancement of knowledge concerning the development, interrelation, and enduring character of the Jewish languages in their manifold manifestations. It is further intended that the Institute shall serve as a treasury (otzar) of such materials, thereby securing their preservation and rendering them accessible to future students of philology, history, and cognate disciplines. The spirit and method of the Institute shall be those of sober and dispassionate inquiry, in accordance with the best traditions of learned societies in the City of London, and with due regard to the dignity and historical significance of each of the tongues herein comprised.
